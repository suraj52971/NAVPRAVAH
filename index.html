Background & Vision

Bihar, one of India’s youngest states in terms of demographics, faces a paradox of potential and neglect. While youth represent a powerful force for social transformation, many are constrained by limited exposure, lack of mentorship, training and inadequate access to skill-building platforms. Rural and semi-urban areas, in particular, witness high dropout rates, underemployment, and a disconnect between youth and opportunities in the development sector.

This initiative envisions reversing that trend. We aim to build an ecosystem where young people—whether in school, college, or out of formal education—can discover their strengths, engage with their communities, and envision themselves as leaders and changemakers. Through structured guidance, real-world exposure, and sustained mentorship, we hope to nurture a generation that is not only skilled but also socially conscious and civically engaged.

Our Mission

Our mission is to empower the youth of Bihar through experiential learning, skill development, and values-based mentorship. By creating pathways for exposure to the nonprofit and development sector, we strive to:

  • Build foundational professional and life skills among youth.
  • Foster curiosity, civic engagement, and a sense of social responsibility.
  • Help participants identify opportunities for further education, employment, or fellowships.
  • Cultivate a culture of learning, reflection, and contribution to society.

We believe that when youth are given the right environment, resources, and guidance, they can break cycles of marginalization and become active agents of positive change—not only for themselves but for their communities at large.

Program Structure: Youth Fellowship

The organization's flagship initiative is a six-month Youth Fellowship Program, targeting 20 selected participants from various blocks in Samastipur:

Month 1: Mentorship & Orientation

  • In-depth workshops led by mentors on communication, leadership, teamwork, goal setting, and self-awareness.
  • Basic digital literacy and training in MS Office tools, public speaking, and group collaboration.

Month 2: First NGO Internship

  • Fellows are placed with NGOs working in different domains (education, health, gender, etc.).
  • Learn how civil society organizations function, engage with diverse communities, and understand social impact.

Month 3: Reflection & Advanced Mentoring

  • Fellows return to discuss experiences, refine their understanding, and receive tailored guidance.
  • Focus on values, ethics, and career pathways.

Month 4: Second NGO Internship

  • Fellows are placed in a different domain to broaden their exposure and explore new interests.

Month 5: Skill Development & Preparation

  • Training in resume writing, presentation skills, fellowship applications, and interview preparation.

Month 6: Future Planning & Fellowship Applications

  • Fellows are supported in applying to long-term national fellowships and higher education programs.
  • They are encouraged to build networks and explore careers in development and public leadership.

Outcomes & Impact

By the end of the fellowship, participants will have:

  • Gained hands-on experience in two nonprofit organizations.
  • Developed a suite of soft and technical skills.
  • Connected with mentors and peers across sectors.
  • Applied for advanced fellowships or employment opportunities.
  • Developed a deeper sense of purpose and civic responsibility.

This program will create a ripple effect by building a core group of young, skilled, and socially committed individuals who can become catalysts for change in Bihar and beyond.
